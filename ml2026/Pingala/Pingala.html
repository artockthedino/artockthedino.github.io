Pingala Rhythms

  • Musical rhythms have mathematical connections!
  • Pingala was an Indian poet around 300 BCE.
  • Pingala described the Fibonacci sequence over 1000 years before Fibonacci.

Quarter notes and half notes

  • A quarter note is filled in. It represents 1 beat.
  • A half note is open. It represents 2 beats.
  • For example, the Jingle Bells rhythm is two quarters followed by a half.

Notice, the time signature, \(\substack{4 \\ 4}\), shows 4 beats per measure (and the four underneath says a quarter note represents a single beat). Try tapping out the 4 beats while the Jingle-Bell rhythm plays.

How many 3-beat rhythms?

When filling a measure of \(\substack{3 \\ 4}\) with quarter notes and half notes, there are only 3 possible rhythms.

  • Can you clap out these rhythms? As one person repeatedly, and evenly, counts one, two, three, try clapping on the 1 and 2, but not the three. Or, try clapping on the 1 and 3, but not the 2. Start slowly!

How can we systematically produce the 5-beat rhythms?

Notice, to make all of the 5-beat rhythms:

  • Put a quarter note in front of each 4-beat rhythm.
  • Put a half note in front of each 3-beat rhythm.
